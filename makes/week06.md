II. Research Documentation (approx. 400 words)
Sources Used
The available research collected includes industry reports provided by Cleanview US Data
Center Map and mapping by Brookings Institution of the AI economy. In the case of physical
infrastructure and mining, MP Materials and RCR Wireless reports on critical minerals were
used to find the reference of information.
The Challenge of Hidden Data
The hardest information to locate was the specifics of a content moderation facility location.
Although corporate offices are transparent, the very buildings in which low- paid employees
sieve traumatizing information are often concealed behind third-party reveals or NDAs. This
displays the outsourcing of safety as a way of ensuring that the AI companies have a clean brand
image and the human psychological cost of AI maintenance is concealed.
Surprising Insights
The sheer scale of the
IREN
Childress, Texas, facility was unexpected. It is an example of a transition, in which the once-
called crypto mines are being re-purposed in large amounts into AI factories since they have
already the giant electrical permits needed to support high-density computer operations.

III. Artist Statement (approx. 250 words)
The Argument
According to this map, AI is not a cloud of code, which exists in an abstract form, but a machine
in the industry that needs resources. The map reveals a geography of extraction by locating the
best HQs of high-end in Silicon Valley and the open-pit lithium mines in Nevada and data
centers in rural Iowa. The physical connection between the intelligence of a chatbot in San
Francisco and environmental degradation of a mine and the gigantic electric load of a rural data
center is physical.
Alternative Perspectives
The mapping of undersea fiber-optic cables around the world may put another power relationship
into the limelight: geopolitical bottlenecks. Such a strategy would demonstrate how select cities
of the coast line dictated the circulation of all the information around the world rather than
concentrating on land-based exploitation.
Connection to Data Feminism
The principle of Data Feminism that is applied to this project is Making Labor Visible. Only the
Standard AI maps include the existence of innovation hubs. With the labor annotation locations
in New Jersey and mining location in California, this map attacks the concept of the AI being
neutral since it reveals the people who create it and the cost. It shifts the emphasis on the
"algorithm" to the bodies and lands that are the support of the former.
