1. What Is Being Made?
● What is the output of this project?
(Map, image, network, visualization, text, interface, etc.)
● What experience does it create for the user?
What is being produced within this particular project is a realistic representation
of a human face that is not associated with any actual human being. What is
produced on a user level is a singular image of a face, although each time the
user refreshes the webpage, a new image is produced. The level of experience is
clearly intended to be somewhat bland and uncomfortable as it is meant to be a
representation of a photograph, while also being a means of addressing the fact
that this human being does not and has not ever existed.

2. What Is the Project Made From? (Data)
● What counts as “data” here?
● Where does it come from?
● What seems missing or excluded?
The information within the project involves millions of photographs of human
faces that are used to train the model. This information seems to be from large
datasets gathered from the internet. What seems to be quite lacking within this
information provided is the experiences and identities of the human beings
whose photographs were used in the creation of the project. Another deficiency
in the provided information is the lack of non-dominant human faces in relation to
generating human faces in the project.

3. Tools, Algorithms, or Systems
● What software, platforms, or models are involved?
● Is AI used? If yes, where in the process?
● What steps feel automated vs. guided?
The model utilized here is known as a Generative Adversarial Network, an
AI-based image generation model. The role of AI within the image generation
process is direct; its generation of faces is automated. The decision to implement
an AI model and to create an image generation website are also examples of
human decision-making.

4. Human Labor & Decisions
● Who likely collected the data?
● Who cleaned, categorized, or interpreted it?
● Where do you see human judgment shaping the outcome?
The human element is likely responsible for creating the facial image data set
used in the project and laying out the algorithm employed in creating the images.
Although the resulting image is created using machines, there is the human
element incorporated in the system used in the project.

5. Design as Argument
● How do visual or interface choices shape meaning?
● What does the project make easy to see?
● What does it make hard to see?
The simple presentation of the website itself lends credibility to the images, which in
turn helps make the images more realistic. Overall, the project works well in showing
just how realistic images produced by AI can be. However, it can make it harder to see
the hidden data, ethical issues, and labor behind the image.


Respond to this week’s reflection prompt in 200–300 words.

In This Person Does Not Exist, “making” in the era of AI focuses less on manually
creating an image and more on developing the system that generates it. The project’s
creators make important decisions by choosing the training data, developing the model,
and determining the presentation of each face on the website, while the computer
produces the final image. This illustrates how creating with AI changes creativity from
direct production to design and planning of systems. A significant benefit obtained from
this process is increased speed and realism. The platform can create countless realistic
faces in moments, demonstrating the strength of AI and the ease with which individuals
trust authentic looking images on the internet. It also prompts users to consider how
frequently we take images at face value merely because they appear credible.
Nonetheless, the project also uncovers what could be sacrificed when machines are
involved in creation. Even though the faces look human, they lack identity, history, or
personal experience. This may simplify people into visual patterns rather than intricate
individuals. The authorship remains ambiguous, as the image is produced by a machine
yet influenced by numerous human choices that go unnoticed. The straightforward
design conceals the work, data, and ethical issues associated with developing the
system. In sum, This Person Does Not Exist illustrates that creation in the era of AI is a
collaborative effort between humans and machines, broadening creative opportunities
while provoking significant inquiries regarding authenticity, trust, and representation.
