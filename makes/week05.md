GPT Analysis (Literary or Rhetorical):
GPT Literary Analysis: “In The Raven, Edgar Allan Poe explores the psychological experience
of grief as a slow descent into obsession. The poem centers on a grieving speaker who mourns
the loss of Lenore and seeks comfort in books and rational explanation. However, when the
raven enters his chamber, the external event quickly becomes internalized. The bird functions
less as a literal creature and more as a manifestation of the speaker’s unresolved sorrow. The
repeated word “nevermore” becomes devastating because it gives final form to what the
speaker fears most: that his loss is permanent. At first, he treats the raven’s speech as
coincidence or mimicry. Gradually, he begins to interpret its answers as deliberate and
prophetic. The tragedy of the poem lies not in the raven’s presence, but in the speaker’s need to
continue asking questions whose answers he already knows will wound him. His grief turns
self-destructive. The chamber setting intensifies this isolation. The entire poem unfolds in a
closed, dim space, suggesting a mind trapped within itself. By the final stanza, the raven’s
shadow stretches across the floor, symbolizing how sorrow has become permanent. The
speaker is no longer simply mourning Lenore; he is imprisoned by memory. Ultimately, the poem
portrays grief not as a moment of sadness, but as a condition that reshapes perception,
language, and identity.”
