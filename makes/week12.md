# Week 12 – Futures of AI & Humanity


Protocol 84-C: Standardized AI Co-Habitation Guidelines (Mandatory Public Notice
Booklet, Year 2093)

1. The Speculative Artifact
Title: Protocol 84-C: Standardized AI Co-Habitation Guidelines (E-Booklet)
Future Date: September 22, 2093
Origin: The Global Bureau of Algorithmic Integrity (GBAI)
Description: This artifact is a e-booklet public notice which substituted all commercial
advertising in transit hubs following the 2038 Energy Rationing Act. Although it is reminiscent
of the early 2020s friendly corporate aesthetics, it is a legally binding directive. Mindful AI is no

longer a proposal, in 2093 it refers to state-regulated thinking filters that are mandatory on all
personal devices to check real-time Carbon Debt.

2. Context Statement
The Future Depicted
The year is 2093. After the Great Compute Wars, and the following Grid Collapses of the
late 2030s, the GBAI was formed as the ultimate international body to make sure that AI does
not drain the rest of the energy resources on the planet. This artifact depicts a world that has
reached an Algorithmic Harmony by being extremely standardized. The friendly, wholesome
faces of the poster mask a harsh truth where compute power is an apportioned resource like
water and all AI lacks autonomy, unmonitored operations.
Present Trends Leading Here
This document terminalizes the existing ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance)
and AI Safety trends. What started in 2024 as voluntary corporate codes of ethics have been
taken up as the hard criminal code of 2093. The poster is a clever use of historical carbon
statistics of the year 2023, namely the five-car emission statistic, as a scare tactic that is
trauma-based in order to make sure that citizens are compliant in their state-approved Compute
Quotas. We are no longer in the Wild West of the early LLMs but in a highly controlled and
regulated 'Walled Garden' under the care of worldly bureaucracy.
Assumptions about Human-AI Relations
There is a Managed Co-Dependency relationship that is revealed in the artifact. AI is
considered a harmful utility which should be controlled and not an exploration tool.
Human-centered has taken on a new meaning of human beings being legally liable as
3
Bio-Validators. All decisions made by AI are legally obliged to be signed by a human, in case an
algorithm commits a mistake in a medical or transit context, the human overseer is the
responsible party who will have to reimburse the community labor or debt due to a software
error. Transparent & Explainable AI is not needed to benefit the user understanding, but to
enable the GBAI to audit all private interactions. This paternalistic relationship presupposes that
people cannot be trusted, and AI cannot be trusted. The government should be between people in
order to keep the planet sustainable at the price of individual freedom.

3. Critical Reflection
Desirability and Avoidability
This is a Soft Dystopian future. It is desirable because it has been able to avoid a
complete climate meltdown and rogue AI disasters. But it is a world that is stagnant. The metrics
of the Safe and Robust have successfully killed the weirdness, and the creative spur of early AI
development. This dystopian future can be prevented by focusing on decentralized, open-source
AI in the present day. We should not allow a few large organizations to determine what is safe
but make the instruments of intelligence locally operated and accessible.
What it Reveals about the Present.
This artifact reveals our current naive thinking that we can engineer our way out of
existential risks by creating more appealing infographics and more inclusive branding. It
demonstrates that our present discussion of AI Ethics is commonly just a prelude to
corporate-state surveillance. In the style of art known as Corporate Memphis (flat, colorful
characters) we can see how we now employ aesthetics to make the loss of privacy something
amiable and human-friendly. Surveillance is now being conditioned into us as care.
Necessary Changes
4
To avoid this Bureaucratic Stagnation in the future, we need to change our attitude
towards environmental responsibility. Instead of a behavior control mechanism of compute
power, we should work on creating Small Language Models (SLMs) that can be executed on
local, private hardware. We need to stop the so-called Safety Rhetoric with a central authority
justifying all the thoughts. The so-called Safe future will be precisely what this poster depicts
unless we are able to distance the concepts of "Responsibility" and "Surveillance" immediately:
the world in which all of the digital thoughts are a subject of state conformity.
