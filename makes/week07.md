Network Analysis (300 Words):
The most important node in my network is Microsoft because it connects to different
layers of the AI ecosystem, such as AI model developers like OpenAI, hardware providers like
NVIDIA and AMD, and enterprise consulting companies like Accenture. Thus, Microsoft
becomes a critical intermediary node that connects different layers of the AI ecosystem.
Furthermore, it is important to note that while companies in different layers of the AI ecosystem
are connected to each other, Microsoft connects to different roles within the AI ecosystem. Thus,
it has more influence compared to companies that are only connected to a single role within the
AI ecosystem. There are several identifiable clusters within the network. One of the clusters
includes AI model developers like OpenAI and Anthropic, which are connected to cloud
providers like Microsoft and Google Cloud. Another cluster includes companies that design and
manufacture chips for AI hardware providers like NVIDIA, AMD, and TSMC. Finally, another
cluster includes enterprise and application companies like Accenture, Palantir, and Darktrace
that use AI in different application scenarios. Thus, these different clusters show how the AI
ecosystem is structured and how different companies within these clusters rely on each other.
One of the most interesting aspects of the network is how dependent AI companies are on
infrastructure providers. Companies like OpenAI are considered to be highly visible in the AI
ecosystem; however, they are dependent on cloud providers and hardware companies that are
considered to be invisible but are equally, if not more, powerful than AI companies. What is
missing in the network is how human labor and raw materials are used in the AI ecosystem.
Laborers who are engaged in labeling datasets for AI companies like Scale AI are invisible in
the network. Furthermore, governments and different regulatory agencies are also invisible in
the network despite the fact that they play a critical role in developing AI technology.

Artist Statement (250 Words):
This network shows that power in AI is not held by the companies that make these
models, but rather a more extensive network of infrastructure, hardware, and labor. Companies
such as OpenAI and Anthropic are highly visible for their work in AI models; however, the
network also shows how these companies rely on less visible companies such as cloud
providers and hardware makers. For instance, Microsoft is a highly visible node in the network
for its work in AI models; however, it also connects different layers of the network and has
control over infrastructure and access to AI tools. There are also visible and invisible actors in
the network of AI models. Companies such as OpenAI, Google Cloud, and Microsoft are highly
visible for their work in AI models; however, companies such as TSMC and Scale AI are
invisible for their work in AI models. TSMC makes the hardware that makes AI possible, while
Scale AI uses human labor to label the data for the AI models. These companies are important
for AI models but are invisible for their contributions to AI models. Thus, it shows that being
invisible does not mean that they are not important for AI models. If the network were structured
in a different way, it could show a different story for AI models. For instance, if human labor or
resources were placed at the center of the network, it could show a different story for AI models.
It could show a different story for AI models because it could shift the focus of AI models from
companies to human labor or resources. Thus, it shows that AI models are a complex network
of different forms of power.
