# Week 5 Reflection

Reflect 5: How does AI alter authorship and remix culture? Who owns AI-made art?


AI alters authorship by making it more of a collaborative process and less about the individual. Rather than having a single author, AI-authored work includes the user, the developers who created the AI, and the data that the AI was trained on. This enhances remix culture by allowing AI to rapidly recombine styles and ideas that already exist, but it also clouds ownership.

From a legal perspective, most copyright laws require human intervention, and therefore ownership is contingent on the level of creative control the human had over the final product. However, AI disrupts the concept of originality because it produces something from patterns that already exist, making authorship more of a layered process.
